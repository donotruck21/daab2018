About Us

Amy and I met for the first time on Valentine's Day, 2006 as freshmen at Newport High School. We knew of eachother from a distance but we first met when our mutual friends decided we would go see the Seattle SuperSonics (rest in peace) play at KeyArena as they were holding a Valentine's Day special on tickets. We met, we sat, we watched, and after the game, we drove home. Well, to be factual, our moms picked us up to drive us home, but hey, we were 15 and I hadn't gotten my Learner's Permit yet! And as it turned out, Amy's family only lived a few minutes away from us so my mom, the ultimate wingman that she is, said we would drive her home. On the first day we met, we found ourselves, on Valentine's Day, 15 years old, in the backseat of my mom's car, napping as we headed home. I know - romantic, right?


We remained friends throughout high school, but we'll fast-forward a few years to when we were both studying at the University of Washington. Amy and I grew much closer as friends through college, going to Mariner's games, the Bite of Seattle, even a trip to NYC with our friend Hannah. It was a short while until I began falling for Amy, but I was at a loss for what to do. How do you tell your best friend that you love her? So naturally, I set up camp in the friend zone. For over 2 years, I secretly pined after Amy while taking up the office as Mayor of the friend zone. But brothers, witness: it is not forever. In the Fall of 2015, Amy confided that she had feelings for me too. We were dating by Winter.


Since then, Amy and I have been paving the way for our wedding day. We are so thankful to our family and friends who have supported us and loved us through the years. We are forever grateful and can't wait to celebrate our big day with you all. Most of all, we thank God for bringing us together in his sovereignty and fostering our relationship into what it is today. We can love and forgive because he first loved and forgave. Praise be to Him!


He is before all things, and in him all things hold together - Colossians 1:17
